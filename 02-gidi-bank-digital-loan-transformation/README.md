# Digital Loan Application Transformation — GIDI Bank

A process transformation case study designing the end-to-end redesign of a loan approval process — from current-state pain points through a phased digital rollout, stakeholder alignment plan, and ROI business case.

## My Role

Approached as the Operations Manager owning the initiative end to end: the current-state to future-state process redesign, cross-functional requirements coordination across IT, Risk & Compliance, and Customer Experience, the KPI framework leadership uses to track delivery, and the business case used to secure funding and sequence the rollout.

## Business Context

GIDI Bank's loan approval process is paper-based and manual, with approvals routinely taking 15+ days. Rising operational costs, customer dissatisfaction, and attrition to faster digital competitors are pushing the bank toward a fully digital application and approval platform.

## Approach

The case study builds out a full transformation plan, not just a list of desired features:

- **MoSCoW-prioritized requirements** — functional requirements ranked Must / Should / Could / Won't, so scope decisions are explicit rather than implied
- **Stakeholder analysis & RACI matrix** — six stakeholder groups (customers, loan officers, operations, risk & compliance, IT, senior management) mapped against responsibility for each major activity
- **Current-state and future-state process flows** — visualized as actual process diagrams, not bullet lists
- **KPI measurement plan** — every target tied to a measurement method, an owner, and a review cadence
- **Integration & technical architecture** — the six systems the platform needs to connect to (core banking, credit bureau, document management, e-signature, notifications, identity verification)
- **Four-phase implementation roadmap** — Q1–Q4 2026, foundation through scale
- **Cost-benefit analysis & ROI** — illustrative investment and benefit figures with an estimated payback period
- **UAT & acceptance criteria** — testable conditions for each major requirement

## Change Management

Digitizing the process only works if people actually adopt it, so change management is treated as a scoped, owned part of the plan rather than an afterthought:

- **Named as a risk with an owner** — "employee resistance to change" sits on the risk register with a specific mitigation (structured training and change management) owned by the Operations Manager, not left as a vague concern.
- **Sequenced into the roadmap** — Phase 3 (Q3 2026) is dedicated to pilot rollout, full paper-to-digital migration, *and* staff training and change management, before Phase 4 scales to all branches. Adoption work happens before scale, not after.
- **Built into stakeholder engagement** — the stakeholder analysis pairs each group's key concern with a specific engagement approach (e.g., loan officers get a digital review workspace *and* hands-on training, not just a new tool dropped on their desk).
- **Reflected in customer-facing design** — "customer adoption barriers" is treated as its own risk, mitigated through intuitive UX design rather than assuming customers will adapt unaided.
